Studies

I'm French and studied in France. For my High School degree (baccaluréat) I specilized in literature and foreign languages. More specifically, I presented both the French and German high school diplomas (baccalauréat and abitur) and got both. I also earned some fancy English degrees on my way. I then started my studies. I did a 2 year long DUT called Service et Réseaux de Communications. The name doesn't mean much, but we studied a lot. We focused heavily on computer science, graphic design and communication skills. But we also had project management, law, art, sound&video lessons, etc. A lot of fields related to the interwebz. Two awesome years. I did learn a lot of things, but most topics were covered quite loosely. The idea was to use the basics taught in class to further expand your skills by working on your own. that worked fairly well for me, and I spent a year and a half coding in Flash, PHP, and creating 3D models in 3DSMax. I did a final year of studies - something called a "license pro", basically one more year after a DUT to get you a bachelor degree. They are tailored to suit companies needs and make you an effective employee right out of the gates.
